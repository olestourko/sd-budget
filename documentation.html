Documentation

SDBudget keeps track of a rolling surplus: your "fun" money.[0]
It assumes that you already have a general idea of what your monthly budget looks like.

Regular Workflow

1. Start of the month: Prepare your budget

Create your budget by estimating your revenues and expenses. [1.1] Decide on how much you want to save this month, [1.2] how much debt you want to repay, [1.3] and how much you want to invest. [1.4]

2. During the month: Enter adjustments

Record transactions which aren't covered in your budget categories. [2.1] [2.2] The more transactions you record, the more accurate the end-of-month estimate is. However, you don't have to record everything. Its entirely up to you how accurate you want to be.

3. End of the month: Close the month

Mark the month as closed [3.1] and enter the closing balance from your bank account. [3.2]

Your First Month

At the start of your first month using SDBudget, you need to set your opening account balance. [4.1]

Optionally, you can also set your opening surplus. [4.2] This may be useful if you are making the transition from a similar budgeting system.
